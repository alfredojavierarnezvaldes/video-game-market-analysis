# video-game-market-analysis

Video Game Market Structural Analysis

# Project Overview

This project analyzes historical global video game sales data to identify long-term market trends, structural shifts, and competitive dynamics within the physical retail segment of the industry.

The objective is not only to describe sales evolution but to evaluate changes in market structure.


# Dataset

Source: Kaggle – Video Game Sales Dataset

Scope: Global physical retail sales

Variables analyzed: Year, Platform, Genre, Publisher, Global Sales

-  Note: The dataset primarily reflects physical sales and does not fully capture digital distribution revenue.


# Methodology

The analysis includes:

Data cleaning and preprocessing

Aggregation of total sales by year, genre, and platform

Comparative platform performance (total vs average sales)

Identification of flagship title concentration

Visualization of structural trends over time


# Key Findings

The industry reached peak recorded sales during the late 2000s.

Sales decline after the peak occurs gradually rather than abruptly.

Market concentration increases significantly after 2015.

A small number of blockbuster titles capture a disproportionate share of sales.

Platform lifecycle and genre preference strongly influence performance.

The combination of declining recorded sales and rising concentration suggests structural consolidation within the physical retail segment rather than a sudden market collapse.


# Structural Insight

Post-2015 data indicates a shift toward higher concentration levels, consistent with industry-wide digital transformation dynamics.

Because the dataset underrepresents digital distribution, the observed decline may reflect redistribution of channels rather than contraction of total industry revenue.


# Limitations

Digital sales and microtransactions are not comprehensively represented.

Analysis focuses on unit sales rather than profitability.

Results apply primarily to the physical retail segment.
